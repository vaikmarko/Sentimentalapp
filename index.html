THE PROBLEM

Why Traditional Memory Preservation Fails

Current approaches to documenting our life stories leave us with fragmented, context-free glimpses that fail to capture what truly matters.

Memory Fades

We lose 40% of emotional context within 24 hours of an experience, making retrospective documentation incomplete.

Documentation Is Fragmented

Photos, videos, journals, and social media exist in isolated silos, creating disconnected snapshots instead of cohesive stories.

Creating Is Time-Consuming

Writing memoirs or organizing life stories requires skills and time most people don't have in their busy lives.

Context Gets Lost

The "why" behind moments disappears, leaving hollow documentation that fails to capture what made experiences meaningful.
